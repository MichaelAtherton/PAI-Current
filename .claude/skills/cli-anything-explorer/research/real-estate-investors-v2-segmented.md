## Strategic Insights

### 1. Excel Is the Universal Bottleneck
Every segment — from note investors cracking tapes to fund managers calculating waterfalls — relies on Excel for mission-critical workflows. LibreOffice Calc (headless, open source) is the single most important CLI-Anything target.

### 2. The "Last Mile" Integration Gap
Most segments have decent software for individual tasks, but moving data between systems is always manual. CLI-Anything pipelines that bridge these gaps are transformative.

### 3. Document Generation Is Universally Manual
Investor decks, LOIs, offering memorandums, marketing materials, quarterly reports — all require pulling data from one system and formatting it somewhere else.

### 4. The Smaller the Investor, the Worse the Tooling
Institutional players have Yardi and MRI. Small operators use spreadsheets. The mid-market (10-50 units, 20-100 notes) is most underserved.

### 5. Three Segments Are Marketing Machines
Wholesalers, land flippers, and STR operators spend most of their time on marketing. CLI pipelines that automate content production directly impact revenue.

### 6. AI Adoption Is Uneven
STR and CRE are ahead. Note investing, tax liens, and MHP are barely touched. Lagging segments = highest-impact automation opportunities.

### 7. The Expanded Software Universe Changes Everything
With Photoshop (ExtendScript), InDesign (ExtendScript), AutoCAD (AutoLISP), SketchUp (Ruby), DaVinci Resolve (Python), and FFmpeg now in play, pipeline output quality matches what firms produce with human teams. The credibility ceiling rises from "good enough" to "indistinguishable from institutional."
