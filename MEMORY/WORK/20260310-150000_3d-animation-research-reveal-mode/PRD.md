## Context

Follow-up to the magical reveal experience analysis. The council agreed on building a "stage mode" with a dark canvas where a briefing assembles itself in real-time. The critical gap: the streaming/3D animation layer that makes the briefing assembly feel cinematic rather than just "text loading." Michael says this is beyond his skill level and needs either (a) a tool with MCP/agent integration, or (b) enough knowledge to build a PAI skill around it.

BriefingPro stack: React 19 + Vite + TypeScript. The animation must work in a browser projected on a screen.

### What "3D animation" actually means here
Not 3D modeling or game engines. The need is cinematic motion design in the browser:
- Text streaming with dramatic reveal (sections materializing, not just appearing)
- Possible depth/parallax/spatial effects as content assembles
- Dark stage aesthetic with lighting effects
- Smooth, performant, projector-friendly

### Risks
- Over-engineering toward full 3D when motion design libraries suffice
- Choosing tools that can't be agent-driven (require manual GUI interaction)
- React incompatibility
